# While You Are Coding

Sharpen your mindset and practices during actual coding — blending intuition, testing, design clarity, and defensive programming.

---

## 1. **Listen to Your Lizard Brain**

**Core Idea**: Pay attention to your gut feeling when something feels off.

* That little inner voice is often right — if something seems too complex, fragile, or error-prone, it probably is.
* Don’t ignore warning signs in your code just to move faster.

> Trust your instincts — they’re honed through experience.

---

## 2. **Programming by Coincidence**

**Core Idea**: Don’t rely on luck or mysterious behavior.

* Avoid code that "just happens to work".
* Understand **why** your code works — don't move on unless you can explain it.

> Replace coincidence with confidence through tests and clarity.

---

## 3. **Algorithm Speed**

**Core Idea**: Be aware of the cost of your code.

* Know the **time and space complexity** of algorithms and data structures.
* Optimize only when necessary — but know **when necessary is**.

**Tip**: Use real-world inputs and profiling, not just guesswork.

---

## 4. **Refactoring**

**Core Idea**: Continuously improve the design of existing code without changing behavior.

* Refactor in small steps and keep tests running.
* Clean up before it gets too messy — don’t wait for a “cleanup sprint.”

> “Code smells? Refactor.”

---

## 5. **Test to Code**

**Core Idea**: Tests guide and verify your work.

* Write tests early, ideally before or alongside your code.
* Good tests document expected behavior and reduce fear of change.

**Tip**: Prioritize testing core business logic and edge cases.

---

## 6. **Property-Based Testing**

**Core Idea**: Instead of testing for specific inputs, define general properties that must always hold.

* Tools like QuickCheck (Haskell) or RapidCheck (C++) can generate test cases.
* Great for math-heavy or logic-focused code.

**Example**: “Sorting a list should not change its length.”

---

## 7. **Stay Safe Out There**

**Core Idea**: Defensive programming helps you write secure, resilient code.

* Validate inputs, handle errors explicitly, use assertions wisely.
* Protect against undefined behavior, injection, race conditions, etc.

> Think like an attacker; code like a guardian.

---

## 8. **Naming Things**

**Core Idea**: Names are powerful tools for communication.

* Choose **meaningful, precise, and consistent** names.
* Avoid abbreviations, and prefer clarity over cleverness.

**Bad**: `dltTmp()`
**Good**: `deleteTemporaryFile()`

---

## Summary Quote 🧠

> "While you're coding, you’re shaping the future — one decision at a time. Make them deliberately."
