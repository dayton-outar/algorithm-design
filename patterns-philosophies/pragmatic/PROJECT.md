# Before the Project

Foundational practices and mindsets that should be in place **before** any code is written. These tenets underscore the importance of collaboration, realistic planning, and approaching requirements with curiosity and agility.

---

## 1. **The Requirements Pit**

**Core Idea**: Requirements are often **vague, incomplete, or wrong**.

* Users often don't know exactly what they want until they see it.
* Written specs rarely capture the true essence of the problem.
* Instead of blindly implementing what's written, ask **why** and **what problem are we solving**?

> ❗ *"The requirements aren't the requirements."*

**Takeaway**: Engage users, iterate fast, and clarify intent over documentation.

---

## 2. **Solving Impossible Puzzles**

**Core Idea**: Software projects are often like solving puzzles with missing or misleading pieces.

* Instead of forcing a flawed plan, **step back** and reframe the problem.
* Look for patterns or analogies from other domains.
* Embrace uncertainty as part of the creative process.

**Technique**: Try a brute-force approach to gather insight, then refine.

---

## 3. **Working Together**

**Core Idea**: Great software is built through **collaboration**, not heroics.

* Treat all stakeholders as partners — developers, testers, users, PMs.
* Share responsibility and knowledge.
* Use shared tools and transparent communication to keep everyone aligned.

**Tip**: Use whiteboards, diagrams, and discussions to reduce miscommunication early.

---

## 4. **The Essence of Agility**

**Core Idea**: Agility is about **adapting to change**, not following a methodology dogmatically.

* Deliver value early and often.
* Embrace feedback and change without resistance.
* Don't be afraid to throw away what isn't working.

> ✅ *"Be responsive, not reactive. Stay flexible."*

---

## Summary Quote 🧠

> "The most dangerous assumptions are the ones we don't know we're making."
