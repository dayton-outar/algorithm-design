# Number Theory

**Based on:** Hardy, G. H., & Wright, E. M. (2008)._An Introduction to Number Theory, Sixth Edition_. Oxford University Press.

**Study assistant:** GPT LLM

Number theory is the study of the integers—the most basic objects in mathematics—and the patterns, structures, and relationships that arise from them. What begins with simple questions about divisibility and prime numbers quickly develops into a rich and far-reaching discipline, where deep truths emerge from seemingly elementary ideas.

At its core, number theory seeks to understand how numbers are built, how they interact, and how they are distributed. Concepts such as primes, congruences, and Diophantine equations provide the foundation, while more advanced tools reveal hidden regularities and surprising connections across different areas of mathematics.

Despite its abstract nature, number theory is both ancient and modern. It carries problems that have fascinated mathematicians for centuries, yet continues to evolve with new methods and applications, from cryptography to computational mathematics. It stands as a testament to how profound complexity can arise from the simplest of beginnings: the counting numbers.
